# Abya Yala Cosmovision

> *Las cosmologías originarias del continente, no la importación cultural de Silicon Valley.*

## Why it matters to the protocol

Amaru is built from the soil where Daniel walks (Colombia, Andes, Amazon). The names, the metaphors, the governance model — they reference Quechua, Muisca, Kogui, Náhuatl traditions because:

- It anchors the project against tech-solutionist defaults
- It opens space for non-Western forms of authority and care
- It signals to Latin-American collectives that this is **theirs**, not a translation

## Concrete manifestations

| Element | Tradition | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Amaru** | Quechua | Two-headed serpent — speaks to two worlds. The protocol mediates between human and AI. |
| **Tinkuy** | Quechua | The ritual encounter of opposing forces — where the new is born. The marketplace of quests. |
| **Welt** (each form) | 8 cultures | Each dimension Daniel works in has a Welt form drawn from Abya Yala. |
| **Pachakuti** (future) | Quechua | The world-turning moment — what we name the protocol upgrades that break compatibility. |

## What it is NOT

- It is **not** cultural appropriation. Daniel is from the Andean continent and uses figures already in the public narrative.
- It is **not** ornamental — these are *operative* metaphors that change architectural decisions.
- It is **not** exclusionary — clans from any culture can adopt Amaru. The framing is offered, not imposed.

## Communal rule

No sacred figures whose invocation requires ceremonial permission are used (no Pachamama as a deity name, no specific masks, no ritual songs in protocol names).
